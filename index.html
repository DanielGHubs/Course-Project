Part 1 — Problem Overview

Soil health issues are often invisible until the lawn is already damaged. SoilSense AI aims to detect these early signs through a combination of sensor data and image-based grass analysis.

The Real Problem

Most people struggle to diagnose soil issues because the warning signs appear first in the grass. Problems like nutrient imbalance, compaction, poor drainage, or dehydration can be detected early by analyzing grass texture, color, and patchiness patterns.

The SoilSense AI Concept

A tool combining sensors and an AlexNet classifier could provide real-time soil health monitoring. The camera detects unhealthy grass patterns and recommends treatments such as watering adjustments or nutrient corrections.

Part 2 — Technical Solution

The model uses a simplified AlexNet architecture designed to classify grass health efficiently.

AlexNet Architecture

The simplified AlexNet model uses layers of convolutions, pooling, and fully connected units to analyze grass images. Each layer learns different levels of visual detail, from basic edges to more complex shapes and textures. This structure makes AlexNet effective at turning raw images into meaningful patterns for classification.

How the Data Fits

All images are resized to 224×224 and normalized so they match AlexNet’s input requirements. Because the dataset contains only 200 training photos, augmentation techniques such as flips and color adjustments help increase variety. This makes the model more flexible and better at handling real-world grass images.

Training & Evaluation Pipeline

The training process included experimenting with batch sizes, learning rates, and evaluation metrics to better understand how hyperparameters affect model performance.

The model was trained for 10 epochs while testing different batch sizes and learning rates to see how training settings affect performance. Accuracy and validation results were monitored to ensure the model learned consistently. After training, the model was tested on new images to measure how well it detects healthy and unhealthy grass in practice.
